📍 MALANG CITY · EAST JAVA · INDONESIA

Conflict X

Conflict X is a graffiti collective rooted in the urban landscape of Malang City, East Java, Indonesia. Emerging from hip-hop culture and a fierce DIY ethos, they have become one of Southeast Asia's most recognizable street art voices — using walls as canvases for community identity, regional pride, and urban resilience.

📄 Interview Transcript

[INTERVIEWER]: Tell us about Conflict X and how it started in Malang City.

[CONFLICT X]: We started from nothing — just the streets, spray cans, and a hunger to put Malang on the map. Hip-hop gave us the foundation. The wall was our medium. We wanted people from here to feel proud of where they come from.

[INTERVIEWER]: How does graffiti connect to the city and community?

[CONFLICT X]: The wall is public space. It belongs to everyone. When we paint it, we're saying — this place has a culture, a history, a voice. It's not just decoration. It's a statement of urban identity.

[INTERVIEWER]: What about sustainability in your practice?

[CONFLICT X]: DIY is in our DNA. We don't waste. We use what we have. Leftover cans, recycled surfaces — that's the culture. It's not about consumption, it's about creation with intention.

KEY THEMES

// WHAT CONFLICT X STANDS FOR //

🎵 Hip-Hop Roots

Graffiti as one of the four elements of hip-hop — a global language spoken with spray paint on concrete.

🏙 Urban Identity

Malang City as a canvas. Using art to build a visual identity for their region that residents can be proud of.

🔧 DIY Culture

Self-reliance, minimal resources, maximum impact. The core ethos of graffiti as sustainable creative practice.

🌿 SDG 11 Impact

Transforming neglected urban spaces into vibrant community landmarks — making cities more inclusive and alive.

♻ SDG 12 Practice

Reusing materials, working small-scale, and creating art that doesn't rely on industrial mass production.

🤝 Community Voice

Graffiti as a form of collective self-expression — giving communities ownership over their visual environment.
