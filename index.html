GRAFFITI + SDGs

// ART AS A FORCE FOR SUSTAINABLE CHANGE //

11

Sustainable Cities & Communities

Graffiti is a form of inclusive urban culture — giving youth, marginalized communities, and local artists a voice in shaping how their city looks and feels.

  • Revitalizes neglected public spaces
  • Celebrates local and regional identity
  • Connects youth to urban belonging
  • Example: Conflict X transforms Malang City walls into community landmarks
12

Responsible Consumption & Production

Graffiti art is a low-resource, high-impact creative form — rooted in DIY culture, recycled materials, and small-batch production rather than industrial output.

  • Reuses leftover and recycled spray cans
  • DIY tools reduce industrial waste
  • Supports localized, small-scale art economies
  • Example: Sweet UTX on eco-friendly graffiti practices
